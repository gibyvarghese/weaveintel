Matter & its states

Edited 2m ago · geneWeave AI is here Drag blocks between columns, or use ◀ ▶
◀▶

Matter is anything that has mass and takes up space. It exists in distinct states — solid, liquid, gas, and plasma — and changes between them when energy is added or removed. The arrangement and motion of its particles is what sets each state apart.

Adding heat to a solid loosens its rigid lattice until it flows as a liquid; more energy still frees the particles entirely into a gas. These transitions are reversible — cooling reverses each step.
